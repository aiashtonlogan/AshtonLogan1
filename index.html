Who I Am

Athlete.
By every
definition.

MTSU Oklahoma Colorado Orange Lutheran Punter NIL Athlete

My name is Ashton Logan — a punter who has competed at the highest levels of college football, lining up for Oklahoma and Colorado before bringing my game to MTSU. My journey started at Orange Lutheran High School in California.

Football gave me discipline. The weight room gave me identity. Outside the field, I train obsessively for aesthetics — the kind of physique that reflects the work, not just the sport.

Growing up in Anaheim Hills, California, I learned early that nothing comes without putting in the work. Football took me from Orange Lutheran to the biggest stages in college football — and now I'm pursuing my MBA at MTSU while building something bigger off the field. This is more than a highlight reel. It's a window into the standard I hold myself to — in every rep, every snap, every decision.

The Mindset

What Drives
Everything.

The physical is just the surface. What separates elite from average lives between the ears — and the will to protect it at all costs.

01
Pressure Is a Privilege
The moments that define careers are the ones most people run from. 4th-and-1 with the game on the line. A 6 AM lift when nobody's watching. Pressure doesn't reveal weakness — it reveals preparation.
02
Process Over Outcome
Four programs. Hundreds of reps nobody saw. The scoreboard doesn't always reflect the work — but the work always shows up eventually. I trust the process of doing the right thing every day, whether it's recognized or not.
03
Standard, Not Mood
I don't have good days and bad days. I have a standard. Some days it's harder to hit — but the standard doesn't move based on how I feel. That's what separates people who want it from people who have it.
