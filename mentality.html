Chapter 02

FEAR is the compass.

"

"The thing you're most afraid of doing is usually exactly what you need to do next."

— Ashton Logan

Transferring four times. Starting over at Oklahoma. Starting over at Colorado. Most people called it instability. I called it proof that I wasn't afraid of discomfort.

Fear tells you where the edge is. Elite athletes don't avoid the edge — they learn to work there. Every program I joined felt impossible at first. That feeling was the point.

The fear of failure on a punt, on a rep, on a business decision — that's not a warning sign. It's a signal that it matters. And things that matter are worth doing scared.
